Summary
  • Summary
    • Stage 2 palliation of single ventricle (also known as Bidrectional Glenn - BDG) involves the creation of a superior cavopulmonary anastomosis to provide pulmonary blood flow
    • Blood flow pathway
      • Single ventricle ejects blood systemically through the neo-aorta
      • Venous blood from head, neck and arms is routed to the pulmonary artery via the superior cavopulmonary anastomosis
      • Oxygenated blood returns via the pulmonary veins to the common atrium
      • Venous blood from the lower body drains via the inferior vena cava (IVC) to the common atrium to mix with oxygenated blood returning from the lungs
  • Physiologic Goals
    • Target saturations should be 75-85%
    • Low-normal pulmonary vascular resistance (pulmonary blood flow is passive)
    • Maintain preload, NSR, normal/low normal SVR
