Other Considerations
  • Special Surgical Considerations
    • General: consider lower body venous access if bleeding anticipated
    • Laparoscopic
      • Pneumoperitoneum has several physiologic effects
        • Carbon dioxide absorption increases PVR
        • Ventilation impairment from increased basal atelectasis increases PVR
        • Increased afterload
        • Decreased preload from reduced IVC venous return can result in hypotension
        • Increased heart rate
      • Limit insufflation pressures (ideally less than 10 mmHg) and total time; consider arterial line to monitor
      • Careful attention to oxygenation and ventilation
    • Neurosurgery: high venous pressures (due to SVC connection to pulmonary artery) predispose patients to increased bleeding risk
