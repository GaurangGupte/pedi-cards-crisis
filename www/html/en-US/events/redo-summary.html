Summary
Preoperative preparation
  • Chart review
    • Previously placed bypass grafts, RV to PA conduit, aortopexy
    • Prior injury to cardiac and extracardiac structures
    • Prior gortex or other membrane placed
  • Risk Assessment
    • Levels of risk - based on CTA findings but confirmed with surgeon
      • Aorta or conduit adherence or erosion into the sternum
      • Hypertensive, dilated RV abutting sternum
      • Dilated or aneurysmal aorta or coronary
    • Structures at risk
      • Innominate vein (especially if pacing leads present)
      • Dilated RV (PHTN)
      • RA, PA
      • Aorta, coronary arteries
  • Preparing for cannulation
    • Site identification
      • Femoral artery/vein
      • carotid artery/internal jugular vein
      • innominate artery/internal jugular vein
    • Vessel patency evaulation
      • Internal jugular vein
      • Axillary artery
      • Femoral or iliac vein
      • Femoral or iliac artery
    • Cannulation strategies
      • Peripheral cannulation and CPB initation before reentry
      • Cutdown on vessels before reentry
      • Wire placement before reentry
  • Anesthesia
    • Large bore IV, RIC or sheath, rapid infuser (Belmont or Level I) for greater than 40 kg, pressure bag, arterial line, CVL
    • Blood in the room before incision
    • TEE in place
    • Cell saver available, perfusionist in room
    • Inotropes and vasopressors primed and read to start before incision
    • Ice available (e.g. need for circulatory arrest)
    • Neuro monitors (cerebral oximetry +/- EEG)
    • Notify blood bank of potential need for large volume pRBC or massive transfusion protocol activation (larger patients)
