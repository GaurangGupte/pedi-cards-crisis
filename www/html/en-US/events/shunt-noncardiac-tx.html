Treatment
  • Acute Intraoperative Hypotension
    • Check for rhythm/ST changes; if not in NSR, consider calling EP
    • IV fluid administration
    • Inotropes may be necessary - epinephrine, dopamine, calcium
    • Check ABG/VBG for anemia and acidosis - treat if found
    • Consider excessive pulmonary blood flow - lower FiO2 to keep saturations greater than 80% and avoid hypocarbia
    • Echocardiography for refractory hypotension
    • Consider other causes:
      • Excessive sedation
      • Anaphylaxis
      • Drug error
      • Bleeding
  • Acute Intraoperative Hypoxemia
    • Check from machine to patient:
      • 100% FiO2
      • Check vent settings and connection
      • suction ETT
      • listen for breath sounds
      • considier FOB or CXR to aid diagnosis
    • Check blood pressure, rhythm (if not NSR, call EP)
    • Consider inotropic support (epinephrine or calcium)
    • Auscultate the chest for presence of a shunt murmur. If none, try to evaluate with echocardiography. If profound desaturation, give empiric heparin bolus (~75-100U/kg). If there has been surgical blood loss, consider giving blood. If not, consider crystalloid or albumin.
    • Peform maneuvers to ensure PVR low
      • increase FiO2
      • moderate hyperventilation
      • check for acidosis and treat if present
      • consider inhaled nitric oxide administration
