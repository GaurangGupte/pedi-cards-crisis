Immediate Actions
    Definitions
    • Mild (Na > 125): usually asymptomatic
    • Moderate (Na 120-125): confusion, muscle cramps, lethargy, anorexia, nausea
    • Severe (Na < 120): seizures, coma
    Types
    • Hypertonic (mOSM/kg > 295)
      • Severe hyperglycemia with dehydration
      • Mannitol administration
      • Radiocontrast administration
    • Isotonic (mOSM/kg 280-295)
      • Pseudohyponatremia
      • Hyperlipidemia
      • Hyperproteinemia
    • Hypotonic (mOSM/kg < 280)
      • Hypovolemic
        • Urinary Na > 20 mEq/L
          • Cause: Renal loss (diuretics, Addison's disease)
          • Treatment: isotonic saline
        • Urinary Na < 20 mEq/L
          • Cause: Extrarenal loss (vomiting, diarrhea, 3rd spacing)
          • Treatment: isotonic saline
      • Euvolemic
        • Urinary osmolality > 100 mOsm/kg
          • Cause
            • SIADH
            • Hypothyroidism
            • Adrenal insufficiency
            • Stress
            • Amphetamines
          • Treatment: fluid restriction and treat underlying cause
        • Urinary osmolality < 100 mOsm/kg => fluid restriction
          • Cause: primary polydipsia, low solute intake
          • Treatment: fluid restriction
      • Hypervolemic
        • Urinary Na > 20 mEq/L
          • Cause: renal failure
          • Treatment: fluid and sodium restriction
        • Urinary Na < 20 mEq/L
          • Cause: heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrosis, hypoalbuminemia
          • Treatment: diuresis, fluid and sodium restriction
