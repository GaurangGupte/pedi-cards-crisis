Other Considerations
  • Considerations for Anesthesia
    • if need CPR, ensure minimul interruptions for compressions
    • Manage airway with ETT if possible, EtCO2 monitoring
    • Anticoagulation
      • Heparin ALWAYS in the ECMO circuit
      • May given patient heparin bolus (50-100 Units/kg) or not, based on situation
    • Sedation and or paralytics as necessary
    • Consider decreasing or stopping pressor/inotropes once cannulated to ECMO due to risk of hypertension once full flow acheived
    • Advise TIME OUT prior to initiation of ECMO to ensure appropriate connections are made (A-A, V-V), heparin administered
  • Considerations for Congenital Heart Disease
    • Aortic insufficiency can cause myocardial distension; LV/LA vent may result in circular shunt
    • Aortopulmonary shunts - PDA, PDA stents, BTTs, MAPCAs
      • Can result in pulmonary congestion
      • Can be modulated with ECMO flows - inotropes can be used to preserve ejection, but may need to control surgically; risk of PA thrombosis if no flow to the lungs
    • Mustard, Senning, Fontan - central cannulatio
      • Single cannula may drain well from IVC
      • Cannula in the baffle may obstruct and not drain well
    • Glenn
      • RA cannula will decompress heart
      • Need to decompress head with SVC cannulation
      • Neck cannulation: need to be careful no to injure the PAs
    • Stage I: No need to control the Sano
